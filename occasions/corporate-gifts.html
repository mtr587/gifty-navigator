🔍 The Office Gift Mismatch

🎁 Most Given

  1. Joke / Funny Gifts — Secret Santa, $15–25
  2. Mugs / Candles / Socks — $10–20
  3. Desk Toys / Gadgets — $15–30
  4. Gift Cards — $15–35

VS

🎯 Most Wanted

  1. Gift Cards — universally appreciated
  2. Useful Desk Items — practical wins
  3. Gourmet Snacks / Treats
  4. Team Group Gift (for bosses)
  5. NOT: Joke gifts (mixed reception)
💡

The Insight: Joke gifts are polarizing — some love them, others find them cringey. Gift cards are the workplace MVP: professional, universally liked, no awkwardness. For bosses: never give an individual gift if you're a direct report — it reads as brown-nosing. Always go with a team group gift. The hierarchy rule: boss → employee gifts are fine. Employee → boss alone = awkward.

💡 Office Gift Rules

1. Down is safe, up is tricky. Boss giving gifts to employees = fine. Employee giving a solo gift to boss = awkward. Always use team group gifts for bosses.
2. Stay under $25 for Secret Santa. Most offices set a $20-25 cap. Respect it — going over makes others uncomfortable.
3. Avoid overly personal gifts. No perfume, clothing, or anything that implies you know their body/size. Keep it professional.
4. Gift cards > joke gifts. Not everyone has the same humor. A $15 coffee gift card is appreciated by 100% of recipients.
