🔍 Why "Just Because" Gifts Hit Different

The psychology of spontaneous gifts is powerful: when there's no social obligation, the gift feels more genuine. The key is that it should show you know the person — reference an inside joke, a conversation, or a small preference they mentioned.

❌ Avoid: Generic gift sets, anything that feels like it was bought in a rush, gifts that create obligation
✅ Aim for: Something they mentioned wanting, a book they'd love, their favorite snack, a small inside-joke item

💡 "Just Because" Gift Tips

1. Timing is everything. "Just because" gifts work best when they're truly unexpected — not right after a fight or when they're expecting something.
2. Reference something specific. "Remember when you said you loved earl grey tea?" = points. "Here's a random gift basket" = confusion.
3. Keep it small. A spontaneous $10 gift creates more joy than an expected $50 one. Don't make them feel indebted.
4. The note matters more than the item. "Saw this and thought of you" on a sticky note > an expensive gift with no context. The note IS the gift.
