🔍 The Thanksgiving Host Gift Mismatch

🎁 Most Given

  1. Wine / Spirits — $15–40
  2. Flower Arrangements — $20–50
  3. Gourmet Food / Chocolate — $15–35
  4. Candles — $10–25
  5. Seasonal Baked Goods / Pie — $10–30
VS

🎯 Most Wanted

  1. Wine (always welcome)
  2. Dessert (bringing a pie is a power move)
  3. After-Dinner Treats (chocolate, coffee set)
  4. NOT: Flowers that need immediate arranging
  5. NOT: Food that needs fridge space or reheating
💡

The Insight: The Thanksgiving kitchen is chaos. Do NOT bring: flowers the host must find a vase for, food requiring fridge space (fridge is already full), or appetizers that need reheating (oven is occupied). Best gifts can wait until after the meal — wine, premium chocolate, or a dessert the host doesn't have to think about. If you bring a pie, you're a hero.

💡 Thanksgiving Host Gift Tips

1. Nothing requiring fridge or oven. The kitchen is at capacity. Bring gifts that sit happily on the counter.
2. Wine is always right. Even if they don't drink, they'll regift or serve it to other guests. Universal safe bet.
3. Skip the flowers. The host is juggling a turkey, 6 sides, and 12 guests. Finding a vase is not on their list.
4. Post-meal enjoyment = best gift. Wine, chocolate, coffee — anything they can enjoy after the dishes are done.
